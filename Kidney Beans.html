Kidney Beans

Learning to garden can be difficult, especially when you live in an urbanized area. Luckily, we've gathered info to help you get started!

Kidney beans were named for the fact that they resemble a human kidney, and are used often in dishes like Chilli Con Carne, and other Mexican dishes.

When do I plant them?: Kidney beans should be planted after the last Spring Frost.

How do I plant them?: Kidney beans are grown from seeds, in loose, well-draining soil that has a pH between 6 and 7. We reccomend you grow from seedlings instead of raw seeds, but both can work. Like the Black bean, it can be grown on a trellis or a bush. Water the plants when it becomes noticeably dry, and harvest when the beans are a yellow hue with a dry touch.. Once you're ready to harvest them, dry them out ina warm, dry spot for a few days, or until the pods harden. Once you remove the beans from the hardened pod, boil them for at least 30 minutes.
