Garlic

Learning to garden can be difficult, especially when you live in an urbanized area. Luckily, we've gathered info to help you get started!

Garlic is an amazing, flavourful plant that can be used in many dishes, and can even help keep aphids out of your garden!

When should I plant it?: Garlic is usually planted in late Autumn or winter, even some varieties in spring! However, we'd reccomend Hardneck garlic, which can survive the cold easier.

How do I plant them?: Garlic is a plant that is best planted in somewhere warm, sunny and fertile, will well-drained soil in a large container (multipurpose compost is best) Use bulb from a garden centre or seed seller instead of the ones you can buy from a supermarket, and break those up into cloves. Plant the large ones 2.5cm deep with the fatter end in the soil. Garlic is a plant that is best suited for high nitrogen feed, such a chicken pellets. You should give this to the plant from April (when you see strong growth). You can harvest them in July, but make sure to sun-dry them for a few days before storing.
